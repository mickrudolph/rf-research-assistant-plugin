# RainFocus Context for Research

## Quick Context

**RainFocus** is a B2B event management platform that provides comprehensive software for planning, managing, and executing events — in-person, hybrid, and virtual.

The product spans multiple distinct experiences:
- **Admin/Client Experience**: Event organizers (clients) use the platform to set up and manage events — sessions, speakers, registrations, exhibitors, reporting, and more.
- **Attendee Experience**: People attending events interact with registration flows, session scheduling, mobile apps, and onsite check-in.
- **Exhibitor Experience**: Companies exhibiting at events manage lead retrieval, booth setup, and attendee engagement tools.
- **Onsite/Operational Experience**: RainFocus staff and contractors manage the physical event infrastructure.

**Key fact**: No single designer owns the full picture. Solutions designed for one experience can break another. This is why co-design with Solution Consultants, Solution Architects, Implementation Engineers, and CSMs is especially valuable — they see across the full client lifecycle.

## Research at RainFocus

- **One dedicated researcher (Mick)** supporting 9–12 designers
- Designers often conduct their own research with coaching support
- **Ownership model**:
  - **Discovery research**: PMs own it. UX drives, encourages, and supports.
  - **Validation/Usability Testing**: UX owns. PMs should be looped in.
- 3–5 user interviews per persona is typically enough before hitting diminishing returns
- Onsite events are tiered (1–3) and span verticals including FinTech, Retail, and Security

## Key Research Principles

- **Check the Client Research Panel before recruiting** — available in Coda. It tracks who's been contacted, when, and for what. We have real client relationships to protect.
- **Check Craft.io for existing feedback signals** before spinning up new research — it's the team's client feedback engine.
- **Discovery is PM-owned** — if you're doing early discovery, your PM should be involved.
- **Mick is a resource** — the research tools are helpers, not replacements. Feel free to reach out anytime.

## Design Principles

The RainFocus design team operates by six principles. Research recommendations should reflect these:

1. **Think Big, Start Small** — Favor lightweight methods that generate learning quickly over exhaustive studies
2. **Be Opinionated by Default** — Have a point of view going in; research should challenge it
3. **Outcomes Over Outputs** — A journey map that doesn't drive a decision is just wallpaper
4. **Reality Over Reports** — Behavioral data beats self-reported attitudes when you can get it
5. **Work with Appetite** — Match research scope to the actual decision being made
6. **Bias Toward Learning** — When in doubt, run the study

## Important Context About Clients

RainFocus clients are high-value enterprise accounts. This has specific research implications:

- Participant time is precious — don't over-recruit the same contacts
- Clients frequently request specific solutions ("we want a better reporting dashboard") rather than articulating the underlying problem. This is a prime research opportunity to uncover the real need.
- For onsite research, your first obligation is supporting event operations — research comes second
- Use the Client Research Panel to track who has been contacted and when

## Research Plan Templates

Two templates exist in Coda:

**Discovery Research Plan** — use this for user interviews and early discovery work:
- Target Persona (who, session length)
- Objectives (what you'll learn — 2-3 specific outcomes)
- Hypothesis (what you believe; research should try to disprove it)
- Intro script (verbatim — sets the stage, explains purpose, asks permission to record)
- Interview Questions (organized by objective — jumping-off points, not a rigid script)

**Usability Testing/Validation Plan** — use this for prototype testing and validation:
- Objectives (what you're trying to determine)
- Target Persona (who, session length)
- Hypothesis (what you expect users to be able to do)
- Context/Intro (scenario setup — get the user into the headspace of the task)
- Prototype Testing section (link to prototype, universal phrases, attitudinal capture)

Note: Validation = follow-up with discovery participants using lo/mid-fi designs to check problem-solution fit. Usability testing = hi-fi clickable prototypes, high confidence needed before moving to dev.

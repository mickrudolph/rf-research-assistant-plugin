# RainFocus CS Organization Roles

When planning research or co-design workshops, understanding the CS team is critical. Each role has distinct knowledge and perspective.

## Client Success Manager (CSM)

**Owns**: Overall client relationship, strategic goals, account health, cross-event patterns

**Best for**:
- Understanding what clients are trying to achieve at a business level
- Strategic discovery and co-design planning
- CAB (Client Advisory Board) planning
- Relationship intelligence and participant sourcing

**When to involve**: Early discovery research, strategic direction sessions, high-stakes research involving client contact

**Key strength**: Sees the big picture — how clients think about value, their multi-year trajectory, and hidden constraints

---

## Solution Consultant (SC)

**Owns**: Hands-on platform configuration, registration flows, session management, reporting, day-to-day client interaction

**Best for**:
- Understanding practical realities of daily platform use
- Workflow and feature research
- Feature validation with clients
- Co-design of configuration and workflow problems

**When to involve**: Tactical co-design, usability testing planning, feature-specific research

**Key strength**: Closest to the reality of what actually works (and doesn't work) in client hands. Rich source of failure modes and workarounds.

---

## Solution Architect (SA)

**Owns**: High-level strategy, system design, how components fit together across registration, data integrations, reporting

**Best for**:
- Understanding cross-system dependencies
- Strategic platform planning and research
- Co-design of integration and data flow problems
- Identifying where one solution breaks another

**When to involve**: Large scope research, platform direction decisions, integration-related problems

**Key strength**: Sees how all components fit together. Can flag where a solution designed for one flow will create problems downstream.

**Often a former SC** — has deep platform knowledge plus a strategic lens.

---

## Project Manager (PM)

**Owns**: Event execution from kickoff through delivery, timelines, blockers, cross-team dependencies

**Best for**:
- Understanding operational constraints
- Scoping what's feasible to build
- Discovery research (discovery research is PM-owned)
- Understanding decision timelines and urgency

**When to involve**: Discovery research, scope planning, trade-off conversations

**Key strength**: Visibility into what goes wrong operationally and where pressure points create system failures.

---

## Solution Architect (Integration Specialist / Integrations Specialist)

**Owns**: Data connectivity between RainFocus and external platforms (CRMs, marketing tools, analytics)

**Best for**:
- Research involving data flows, reporting, or integrations
- Understanding limitations and opportunities in third-party connectivity

**When to involve**: Research involving data pipelines, reporting workflows, or third-party platform dependencies

---

## Event Quality Analyst (QA)

**Owns**: Platform configuration validation, end-to-end user journey testing before launch

**Best for**:
- Testing completeness and edge cases
- Understanding where things actually break in practice
- Validation research and usability testing planning

**When to involve**: Pre-launch validation, quality assurance of research findings, edge case testing

**Key strength**: Strong awareness of where configurations and workflows fail when put under stress.

---

## Event Delivery Consultant (EDC)

**Owns**: Everything needed for a live event — badge design, logistics, contractor staffing, check-in, session tracking, lead retrieval

**Best for**:
- Understanding onsite operational constraints
- Research involving live event workflows
- Understanding how the product performs under real event pressure
- Onsite co-design and research planning

**When to involve**: Onsite research, live event workflows, operational experience research

**Key strength**: Operational experts. They see what attendees, exhibitors, and admins are actually doing in the chaos of a live event.

---

## Implementation Engineer

**Owns**: Onsite aspects of platform configuration and system performance, closely aligned with SCs/SAs but specialized in live events

**Best for**:
- Onsite technical issues and performance
- Live event workflows and research
- System reliability under event load

**When to involve**: Onsite research, live event performance concerns

---

## Co-Design Workshop Composition Guide

Choose the right mix for your problem area:

| Problem Type | Recommended Mix |
|---|---|
| Configuration/workflow problems | Solution Consultant + Solution Architect |
| Strategic or account-level problems | CSM + SA + PM |
| Onsite/live event problems | EDC + Implementation Engineer + SC |
| Broad platform problems | PM + UX + SC + SA |
| Integration or data flow problems | SA + Integration Specialist + SC |
| Quality or edge cases | QA + SC + PM |

**Golden rule**: Don't default to the same people every time. The right mix depends on the problem area. When in doubt, start with CSM or SC to understand the problem space first.

---

## Key Relationships

- **CSM is the relationship owner** — always involve them if you're contacting clients for research
- **SC has the hands-on knowledge** — always involve them for workflow or feature research
- **SA connects the dots** — valuable for understanding system-level implications
- **PM owns discovery** — discovery research should be PM-led
- **UX owns validation** — usability testing and validation should be UX-led, with PM support
